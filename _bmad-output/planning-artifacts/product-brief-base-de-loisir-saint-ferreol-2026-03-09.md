# Product Brief: base-de-loisir-saint-ferreol

## Executive Summary

La Terrasse Saint-Ferréol is the official website for the leisure base operating at Lac de Saint-Ferréol, a landmark lake near Toulouse. The site serves as the digital front door for three service poles — Restaurant, Aventure, and Événements — managed by a single operator under a unified brand. The website must allow visitors (tourists and locals alike) to discover activities, understand the offerings, and book services. It aims to stand out in a sector where web presence is typically dated and minimal, through quality design and a clear, emotionally engaging user experience.

---

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Leisure bases typically rely on outdated, poorly designed websites that fail to convey the quality of the experience on offer. Lac de Saint-Ferréol is a landmark destination, but without a modern digital presence, potential visitors — both tourists passing through and local residents — lack a clear, inviting way to discover what's available, plan their visit, and book activities.

### Problem Impact

Without an effective website, the leisure base relies heavily on word-of-mouth and physical signage. Tourists may pass through without knowing the full range of offerings. Groups and businesses looking for seminar or team-building venues can't quickly assess the space. Cross-selling between the three poles (restaurant, activities, events) doesn't happen, leaving revenue on the table.

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

Competing leisure base websites in the region are typically simple, dated, and utilitarian. They list services without conveying atmosphere, rarely cross-sell between offerings, and provide poor mobile experiences. None leverage the emotional pull of their location as a differentiator.

### Proposed Solution

A modern, well-crafted website built around the principle "the place is the product." The site uses Lac de Saint-Ferréol as its narrative anchor, presenting three poles as distinct but interconnected experiences. Each pole page functions as an autonomous landing page (SEO-optimized), while a double-layer cross-linking system educates visitors about complementary services. V1 is static with booking via contact forms; V2 will add real-time availability and online booking.

### Key Differentiators

- **Sole operator** on a landmark, historically significant lake — no direct local competition
- **Quality gap exploitation** — the bar for leisure base websites is low; a polished, well-designed site immediately signals a premium experience
- **Unified brand, three identities** — sub-brand system (Restaurant/Aventure/Événements) with distinct visual tones under the LA TERRASSE umbrella
- **Location as narrative anchor** — the lake and its surroundings sell the experience before any service description does
- **Cross-selling architecture** — structural + contextual linking between poles, designed to increase per-visit spend

## Target Users

### Primary Users

**1. Sophie — The Family Organizer**
- **Profile:** Parent (30-45), typically the mother, with 1-3 children (ages 4-14)
- **Goal:** Find an outdoor activity for the family, ideally combinable with a meal
- **Behavior:** Searches Google for "activité enfant plein air" or "base de loisir saint ferreol" on a weekday evening or Saturday morning. Browses activity options, checks prices and age suitability, may pivot between activities if the first choice doesn't fit
- **Success moment:** Finds an activity that works for all ages, sees she can also eat on-site, and books or calls in under 5 minutes
- **Volume:** High — families represent the largest visitor segment

**2. Marc & Léa — The Casual Couple**
- **Profile:** Couple (25-40), no kids or kids-free day, looking for a pleasant outing
- **Goal:** Find a nice spot for lunch outdoors, possibly combined with a leisurely activity
- **Behavior:** Searches "restaurant bord de l'eau Toulouse" or "où manger lac saint ferreol". Decision driven by atmosphere and setting, not menu details. May discover activities as a bonus
- **Success moment:** Sees the terrace, the lake, the vibe — and decides "on y va" before even reading the menu
- **Volume:** High — especially in spring/summer tourist season

**3. Laurent — The Corporate Planner**
- **Profile:** HR manager or executive assistant (35-55), organizing a seminar or team-building day for 15-60 people
- **Goal:** Quickly assess if the venue fits (room capacity, equipment, catering, activities) and get a quote
- **Behavior:** Searches "séminaire entreprise Toulouse nature" or "team building plein air". Compares 3-5 venues. Needs specs immediately — capacity, equipment, pricing structure. Decides fast
- **Success moment:** Finds room specs, sees the 3 named packages (Simple/Gourmet/Aventure), requests a quote within 3 minutes
- **Volume:** Lower frequency, but highest average spend per booking

**4. Karine — The Group Organizer**
- **Profile:** Association president, club leader, or group coordinator (40-60), organizing an outing for 15-40 people
- **Goal:** Combine activities + meal for a group (sports club, retirees' association, friends gathering)
- **Behavior:** Needs cross-pole information — activities AND restaurant AND possibly event space. Lands on homepage or transversal pages (/en-groupe). May call directly for custom arrangements
- **Success moment:** Understands the full offer across poles, finds a "journée type" that matches her group's profile, picks up the phone
- **Volume:** Medium — seasonal peaks (spring/early summer)

### Secondary Users

**5. Younger Visitors (18-25)**
- Students or young adults looking for affordable outdoor fun (paddle, mini-golf, trampoline). Discover via social media or word of mouth. Price-sensitive, spontaneous

**6. School Groups & Educational Visits**
- Teachers organizing day trips. Need group pricing, activity descriptions with educational value, logistical info (parking, access). Typically contact by phone or email

**7. Retirees**
- Local retirees or retiree groups looking for a calm outing — restaurant with a view, gentle walk by the lake. Value clarity, readability, and phone contact

**8. The Operator (site admin)**
- Corentin's brother and staff managing content updates (seasonal menus, activity availability, event calendar) via Keystatic CMS. Needs a simple, intuitive back-office

### User Journey

| Phase | Families & Couples | Corporate & Groups |
|-------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Discovery** | Google search, word of mouth, tourism office, on-site signage at the lake | Google search ("séminaire nature Toulouse"), professional networks, tourism office |
| **First contact** | Land on activity or restaurant page (SEO landing), or homepage | Land on Événements page or transversal /en-entreprise page |
| **Exploration** | Browse activities/menu, check prices, discover other poles via cross-linking | Check room specs, compare packages, assess catering + activity options |
| **Decision** | Emotional — atmosphere, photos, "ça a l'air bien" | Rational — specs, pricing, comparison with alternatives |
| **Action** | Call or book online (V2), may decide spontaneously on-site | Request quote via form, follow up by phone/email |
| **Post-visit** | Word of mouth, return visits, seasonal loyalty | Repeat bookings if experience was good, recommend to peers |

## Success Metrics

### User Success Metrics

- **Information findability:** Visitors find activity details, prices, and practical info (hours, location, parking) without needing to call
- **Clear path to action:** Each page leads naturally to a contact form, phone number, or booking action (V2)
- **Cross-discovery:** Visitors arriving for one pole (e.g., restaurant) discover the other poles through cross-linking — measured by multi-page sessions
- **Mobile experience:** Site is fully functional and pleasant on mobile, where the majority of leisure searches happen

## MVP Scope

### Core Features

**Pages & Structure (V1 — mid-April 2026):**

- **Homepage:** Narrative lieu-first — hero Saint-Ferréol → 3 poles → "journée type" creative section → agenda/events → CTA
- **Restaurant hub + sub-pages:** La carte, Nos producteurs, Repas de groupe (tone: warm, sensory, brun terre)
- **Aventure hub + sub-pages:** 10 activity pages (Pédalo, Mini-golf, Paddle, Canoë, Vélo électrique, Vélo classique, Archery tag, Course d'orientation, Jeu de poste, Trampoline) with prices, navigable cards, alternative suggestions (tone: dynamic, fun, vert végétal)
- **Événements hub + sub-pages:** La salle (specs-first), Séminaire Simple, Séminaire Gourmet, Séminaire Aventure (tone: professional, bleu ardoise)
- **Transversal pages:** En Famille, En Groupe, En Entreprise — each with a "journée type" narrative
- **Contact page:** Form + phone + map/directions

**Navigation:**
- Mega-menu: poles + transversal entries by visitor profile
- Mobile: slide-out panel with accordion sub-menus

**Cross-linking system:**
- Layer 1 (structural): permanent "La Terrasse c'est aussi..." block on every page
- Layer 2 (contextual): narrative suggestions per page ("Après votre pédalo, déjeunez au bord du lac")

**i18n:** FR (primary) + EN + ES from launch

**CMS:** Keystatic for content management (menus, prices, seasonal info, activity details)

**Contact/Booking (V1):** Contact form + phone number — no online booking

**SEO:** Each pole page and activity page is an autonomous landing page, optimized for relevant keywords

**Technical:**
- Astro (static-first) + Svelte islands for interactive components
- TailwindCSS with design token system (per-pole colors and tones)
- Mobile-first, < 2s load on 4G
- Content structure built first, filled iteratively as content becomes available

### Out of Scope for MVP

- **Online booking / real-time availability** — deferred to V2
- **Photo gallery** — placeholder space reserved, content added post-launch
- **Testimonials / reviews** — V2 feature, placeholder reserved
- **User accounts / login** — not needed
- **Payment processing** — V1 is contact-based
- **Blog / news section** — not in V1
- **Event calendar with dynamic data** — V1 uses static seasonal info
- **Analytics dashboard** — Google Analytics + Search Console only

### MVP Success Criteria

- Site live and fully functional by mid-April 2026
- All pages accessible, responsive, and fast (< 2s on 4G)
- SEO-optimized: indexable, proper meta tags, structured data
- Content structure complete even if some content is placeholder
- CMS operational: operator can update menus, prices, and seasonal info
- Contact form functional and delivering leads
